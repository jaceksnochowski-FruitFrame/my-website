Cinematography Lab

Hands-On
Shooting Sessions

Most of us do our safest work when a client is watching. That is normal, but it also means you do not always get the space to try things, get them wrong, and learn from them.

Cinematography Lab is a focused space to test ideas properly with experienced practitioners, practical setups, and time to iterate with intention.

We build a scene from a visual reference, shape light, test movement, work with talent when available, then shoot, review, and refine.

It is for cinematographers, operators, and filmmakers who want to spend more time making, testing, and learning on set.

Approach

How I Ended Up
Shooting This Way.

This is probably the simplest way to explain what shapes the work and how I tend to be on set.

I came into cinematography through dance, so I naturally pay a lot of attention to rhythm, timing, and how people move through space. That still affects the way I shoot now, whether it is a dance film, a commercial, or a performance-led piece.

Over time, that turned into something practical on set. I’m usually thinking about how to keep the camera expressive without making it showy, how to support performance, and how to make strong visual decisions without slowing the day down.

I work well under pressure and tend to think fast when something needs solving. Good communication, solid prep, and being able to adapt quickly matter just as much to me as the frame itself.

What that usually means: camera movement that feels motivated, not forced.
On performance-led work: a better instinct for choreography, blocking, and timing.
On commercial jobs: strong visuals, but with an eye on pace, clarity, and getting the work done properly.
About

Dance Came First.

Cinematography Lab

A Separate Space For Filmmakers Who Want To Train Their Eye And Technique.

Cinematography Lab is a side initiative at Fade Street Studios for experienced practitioners who want hands-on testing time away from client pressure.

It sits outside the main DOP offering, but it reflects the same focus on movement, lighting, and practical decision-making on set.

  • Build a scene from a visual reference
  • Light with purpose and adjust in real time
  • Test movement, camera setup, and coverage
  • Review footage, refine, and shoot again
