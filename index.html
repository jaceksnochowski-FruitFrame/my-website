Cinematography Lab

Hands-On
Shooting Sessions

Most of us do our safest work when a client is watching. That is normal, but it also means you do not always get the space to try things, get them wrong, and learn from them.

Cinematography Lab is a focused space to test ideas properly with experienced practitioners, practical setups, and time to iterate with intention.

We build a scene from a visual reference, shape light, test movement, work with talent when available, then shoot, review, and refine.

It is for cinematographers, operators, and filmmakers who want to spend more time making, testing, and learning on set.

About

Dance Came First.
The Camera Followed.

Dance has always been part of how I see cinematography. It shaped the way I understand rhythm, timing, bodies in space, and how movement can carry emotion without forcing it.

I came to cinematography through dance, and that still shapes how I see movement, rhythm, and timing on screen.

I’m a Dublin-based Director of Photography, camera operator, and certified Steadicam and gimbal operator working across commercial, branded, music, theatre, performance-led, short film, and narrative projects.

My approach is built around movement, timing, and control — shaped by a background in competitive breakdancing and years of hands-on operating. That gives me a practical instinct for choreography, rhythm, and how bodies move through frame.

As both a DOP and operator, I focus on creating clean, cinematic visuals that work in real production environments. The aim is not just to design strong images, but to execute them efficiently and consistently on set.

I also co-run Fade Street Studios and FruitFrame, giving me a solid production base and the ability to scale projects with clarity and reliability when needed.

Cinematography Lab

Hands-On Sessions For Filmmakers Who Want Better Control On Set.

Collaborative shooting sessions at Fade Street Studios where experienced filmmakers build scenes, shape light, test movement, and refine practical decision-making without client pressure.

The format is focused and efficient: real space, real equipment, small groups, and enough time to test ideas properly instead of talking around them.

  • Build a scene from a visual reference
  • Light with purpose and adjust in real time
  • Test movement, camera setup, and coverage
  • Review footage, refine, and shoot again

Session Details

  • Duration3 to 4 hours
  • LocationFade Street Studios, Dublin 2
  • GearStudio space and lighting included
  • FormatSmall group, rotating roles, practical focus
  • LevelBest suited to experienced practitioners
